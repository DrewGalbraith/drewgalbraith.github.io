About Me

My Background
I came into STEM from a humanities background. Linguistics was the hardest (read: most empirical) social science I found and I drifted into the harder aspects therein. Though I focus on web development today, I maintain a healthy interest for sociolinguistics and neurolinguistics, including language prestige and stigmatization and the use of EEG to map linguistic events in the brain. Eventually, I found natural language processing (NLP) and discovered I had a knack for manipulating text and for programming more broadly. Before long, I had joined a university lab researching LLMs.

My Philosophy
I am a natural-born skeptic. If I hear a claim, I want to see your work. I'm also skeptical about intentions. I don't think we put tech where it helps the most people, especially those least advantaged by our society. The most meaningful outcome for this work isn't just creating tech, it's creating tech with purpose to raise each other up. I'm inspired by groups like Recidiviz, Oklo, and the Algorithmic Justice League. I hope to be part of a more ethical and responsible tech future.

Outside Coding
In my free time, I like getting out with my family, reading from my book list, and trying to solve games with algorithms, even when doing so makes the games less fun afterward; (see my upcoming Ticket to Ride post).
