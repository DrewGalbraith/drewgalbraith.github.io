About

Research background, guiding ideas, and the work I want to do with language technology.

About Me

My Background
My name is Drew Galbraith. I study large language models. I also have a degree in linguistics, and though I focus on ML today, I maintain a strong fascination for sociolinguistics and neurolinguistics, including language prestige and stigmatization and the use of EEG to map linguistic events in the brain. While working in those spaces as an undergraduate, I was introduced to natural language processing and discovered I had a knack for manipulating text and for programming more broadly. That led me to invest more deeply in technical work, including joining a university lab to research LLMs.

My Philosophy
I want to use LLMs to lift the lives of people least advantaged by technology. The most meaningful outcome for this work is not taking a well-served user from good to slightly better, but helping someone with less access to education, documentation, or economic opportunity cross a much larger gap. That pushes me toward open research, open code, and open model artifacts wherever possible.

Outside Research
In my free time, I like paddle sports, reading from my book list, and playing guitar. I also enjoy trying to solve games with algorithms, even when doing so makes the games less fun afterward.
